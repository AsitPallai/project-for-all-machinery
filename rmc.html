RMC Notes

Ready Mix Concrete (RMC)

Definition: Ready Mix Concrete (RMC) plant is a facility where concrete is produced in a controlled environment by mixing cement, aggregates, water, and admixtures, and then delivered to construction sites in a ready-to-use form.

Types of Mixers Used in RMC

  • Pan Mixer: A mixer using stationary pan with rotating blades, used for uniform mixing. Preferred for precast concrete production.
  • Twin Shaft Mixer: A fast, robust, and homogeneous mixing system.
  • Drum Mixer

Difference Between Twin Shaft Mixer and Pan Mixer

  • Twin Shaft Mixer:
    • Uses two horizontal shafts with synchronised blades that rotate in opposite directions.
    • Efficient for high-volume concrete mixing.
    • Two interlocked shafts ensure faster mixing.
    • More moving parts; requires higher maintenance.
  • Pan Mixer:
    • Features a stationary concrete pan with rotating blades inside.
    • Suitable for smaller production capacities.
    • Simpler and easier maintenance.
  • Both mixers are effective, but twin shaft mixers are chosen for speed, volume, and energy efficiency.

CCB (Circuit Breakers)

CCB (Circuit Breaker): A protective electrical device that interrupts current flow in the event of an overload or short circuit, preventing damage to connected equipment and electrical fires.

Types

  • MCCB (Moulded Case Circuit Breaker): Provides protection against overload and short-circuit faults. Does not offer protection against earth leakage currents.
  • RCCB (Residual Current Circuit Breaker): Detects leakage currents and disconnects the circuit to prevent electric shocks.
  • ELCB (Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker): An older type of device that detects leakage current from the system and disconnects supply to prevent electric shocks and fire hazards.
  • RLP (Residual Leakage Protector): Provides safety from residual leakage by automatically disconnecting the circuit.
  • MPCB (Motor Protection Circuit Breaker): Protects electric motors from overloads and short circuits, automatically switching off faulty connections.

7. Daily Maintenance Checklist

  • Pan mixer – check wear plates, mixing arms, blades
  • Discharge gate – check cylinder operation
  • Load cells – check for zero error
  • Belt conveyor – inspect tension & alignment
  • Skip bucket – check wire rope condition
  • Water pump – check pressure & leakage
  • Air compressor – drain moisture
  • Lubrication – grease all nipples
  • Aggregate bins – check material overflow & jamming
  • Pneumatic line – check air leakage
  • General cleaning – remove concrete deposits

8. Weekly Maintenance Checklist

  • Mixer – check gearbox oil level
  • Mixing arms & blades – torque check bolts
  • Belt conveyor – check bearings & rollers
  • Skip winch – check drum, rope pulleys, grease
  • Skip rope – inspect complete rope
  • Load cells – check calibration deviation
  • Air compressor – check oil level

9. Monthly Maintenance Checklist

  • Mixer gear box – oil change
  • Inspect wear plates
  • Skip rope – health inspection
  • Belt conveyor – check tightness and greasing
